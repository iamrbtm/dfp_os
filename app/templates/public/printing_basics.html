3D printing basics for normal people

If you’ve never bought a 3D printed item before, this page explains the basics without the jargon.

What is a 3D printed item?

It’s a physical object built layer by layer from melted filament. That means tiny layer lines are normal and part of the handmade look.

Will every item look identical?

Close, but not factory-identical. Color transitions, shimmer, and tiny texture differences are part of what makes each print unique.

What material do you use?

Most gifts and desk items are printed in PLA. It’s lightweight, durable for normal indoor use, and available in lots of colors.

How should I care for it?

Avoid extreme heat, especially hot car interiors. Clean with a soft cloth and don’t bend articulated pieces farther than they naturally want to move.

Good to know before ordering

  • Small parts can be a choking hazard for young children.
  • Color-shift and rainbow filaments can vary from one batch or print to another.
  • Custom orders usually take longer than ready-made items because they need quoting, setup, and test prints.
  • If you are buying for a business or event, it helps to order early so there’s time for revisions.
